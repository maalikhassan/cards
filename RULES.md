# LUCK & LOGIC - Game Rules & Instructions

## üìã Game Overview

**LUCK & LOGIC** is a strategic board game that combines chance with skill. Players navigate a game board, draw action and challenge cards, and compete to reach the finish line first or achieve the highest score.

**Players:** 2-6 players  
**Age:** 8+  
**Duration:** 20-45 minutes  
**Difficulty:** Easy to Medium

---

## üéØ Objective

Be the **first player to reach the finish line** while accumulating **the highest score possible** along the way.

---

## üé≤ Setup

1. **Place the board** on a flat surface where all players can see it
2. **Each player chooses a token** and places it on the START square
3. **Prepare the card deck:**
   - Shuffle all 20 Action Cards
   - Shuffle all 10 Challenge Cards separately (or mix together based on difficulty)
   - Place in a central location, face down
4. **Decide turn order** - Roll dice or go in clockwise order
5. **Keep a scorepad** to track points

---

## üéÆ How to Play

### Basic Turn Structure

Each player's turn consists of:

1. **Roll the Dice** - Roll a standard 6-sided die
2. **Move Your Token** - Move forward the number of spaces shown
3. **Land on a Tile** - Perform the action of the tile you land on
4. **Draw a Card** (if applicable) - Follow the card's instructions
5. **End Turn** - Pass control to the next player

### Types of Tiles

**Regular Tile (Empty)**
- No effect; your turn simply ends
- Move to the next player

**Action Card Tile**
- Draw an Action Card from the deck
- Apply the card's effect immediately or keep it for later
- Some cards are "instant," others you can save

**Challenge Tile**
- Draw a Challenge Card
- Attempt the challenge (riddle, math, physical, etc.)
- **Success:** Gain the points indicated
- **Failure:** Lose the points indicated (or nothing happens)

**Penalty Tile (Optional)**
- Lose a certain number of points
- Some may require drawing a penalty card

---

## üé¥ Action Cards

Action Cards provide special abilities that can:
- **Boost your score** - Steal, Gift, Lucky Break
- **Affect other players** - Swap, Force Reroll, Block
- **Modify gameplay** - Reverse, Double Throw, Roll Again
- **Provide defense** - Immunity, Resurrection

### How to Use Action Cards

**Instant Cards** (Use immediately):
- Roll Again
- Double Throw
- Steal
- Score Swap
- And others marked as instant

**Keep Cards** (Save for later):
- Immunity
- Block
- Alliance
- Others you choose to save

*Note: You may only hold a maximum of 3 cards at a time. Discard excess cards.*

---

## ‚ùì Challenge Cards

Challenge Cards require skill to succeed. They include:

**Types:**
- üß© **Logic Puzzles** - Answer riddles and logical questions
- üî¢ **Math Challenges** - Solve equations and number sequences
- üí¨ **Memory Challenges** - Recall information or perform memory tasks
- ü§∏ **Physical Challenges** - Do jumping jacks, hold poses, etc.
- üéØ **Trivia** - Answer general knowledge questions

**Success/Failure:**
- **Correct Answer:** Gain the reward points (+2 to +5)
- **Wrong/Failed:** Lose penalty points (-3 to 0) OR nothing happens
- **Player Choice:** Decide who judges if answer is correct

---

## üèÅ Winning

**The game ends when:**

1. **Any player reaches the FINISH line** - That player wins immediately
   - OR if you prefer, play until everyone finishes

2. **After a set number of rounds** - Highest score wins
   - Recommended: 10 rounds (about 30-40 minutes)

**Tiebreaker:** If two players tie on points, re-roll the dice once.

## üé≤ Dice Rolling

- **Standard die:** Roll once per turn
- **Natural 6:** Some variants allow an extra roll
- **If using ROLL AGAIN card:** Roll again in same turn
- **If using DOUBLE THROW:** Next throw points are x2

## üîÑ House Rules (Optional Variations)

### Beginner Mode
- Use only 15 of the 20 Action Cards (remove hardest ones)
- Reduce Challenge penalties to 0 (reward only)
- Start with +5 bonus points each

### Advanced Mode
- All cards in play
- Keep track of complex card interactions
- Stricter Challenge time limits (10 seconds only)
- Can use one card per turn maximum

### Team Mode (4-6 players)
- Divide into two teams
- Combine team scores
- First team to 50 points wins
- Can strategically help teammates

### Speed Mode
- Shorten board (20 squares instead of 30)
- Play 5 rounds instead of 10
- Reduce Challenge time to 5 seconds

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Important Rules

1. **Card Limits:** Maximum 3 Action Cards per player
2. **Instant Cards:** Must be used immediately unless specified
3. **Card Discard:** Discard if you have more than 3 cards
4. **Duplicate Cards:** Can have multiple copies of the same card
5. **Disagreements:** Player majority decides judgment calls
6. **Round Timer:** Use a phone timer for Physical/Timed challenges

## üéØ Strategy Tips

**For Beginners:**
- Save defensive cards (Immunity, Resurrection)
- Don't use all cards at once
- Watch for opponent's strategy

**For Advanced Players:**
- Combine card effects for power moves
- Predict when others will land on Challenge tiles
- Use blocking cards strategically
- Manage card hand carefully

---

## üìù Variant Rules

### Handicap Rules
- Trailing player gets 1 extra card per turn
- Leader loses 2 points per round
- Youngest player gets 2 free rolls

### Difficulty Levels
- **Easy:** Remove 5 hardest Challenges, no penalties
- **Medium:** All cards in play, normal rules
- **Hard:** Stricter time limits, harder challenges

### Solo Play
- Play against AI (self-determined rolls)
- Try to beat your own best score
- Complete all 30 cards without penalties

---

## üêõ Troubleshooting

**What if I can't answer a Challenge?**
- Best effort counts
- Group votes if unclear
- Generally results in 0 points, not negative

**Can I trade cards with other players?**
- Only if agreed upon by all players
- Recommended: No trading (keeps it fair)

**What if I draw the same card twice?**
- Completely allowed
- Stack effects if possible

**How do I handle ties?**
- Both/all players re-roll once
- Highest roll wins

## üí° FAQ

**Q: Can I save an Action card for later?**
A: Yes, unless it's marked "instant use." Maximum 3 cards total.

**Q: What if someone disagrees about a Challenge answer?**
A: Majority vote decides. If tied, it counts as correct.

**Q: Can I use multiple cards in one turn?**
A: Only one card per turn (unless a card says otherwise).

**Q: Is there a maximum score?**
A: No, unlimited points possible.

**Q: Can I go negative?**
A: Yes, unless you use RESURRECTION card.

**Q: What age is appropriate?**
A: Age 8+ for basic rules. Adjust challenges for younger players.
