# LUCK & LOGIC - Board Game Cards

A printable card deck for the **LUCK & LOGIC** board game. This project includes beautifully designed action and challenge cards with a neon-themed aesthetic.

## üìã Overview

**LUCK & LOGIC** is a fun and interactive board game that combines luck with logical thinking. The game features:

- **20 Action Cards** - Special ability cards that can boost your score, sabotage opponents, or create unpredictable situations
- **10 Challenge Cards** - Brain teasers, riddles, math problems, and physical challenges

## üéÆ Card Types

### Action Cards (20 Total)
Action cards allow players to perform special moves that affect gameplay. These include:
- Score-modifying actions (Steal, Gift, Lucky Break)
- Turn-based effects (Roll Again, Block, Resurrection)
- Strategic plays (Score Swap, Mirror, Alliance)
- Game-changing moves (Reverse, Shuffle, Immunity)

**Action Cards:**
1. **STEAL** - Steal 3 points from any player
2. **SCORE SWAP** - Trade scores with the player to your right
3. **DOUBLE THROW** - Your next throw's points are doubled
4. **IMMUNITY** - Discard to skip the next penalty
5. **ROLL AGAIN** - Take another turn immediately
6. **GIFT** - Give 2 of your points to another player
7. **FORCE REROLL** - Force another player to re-roll
8. **POWER UP** - Gain +1 bonus point for your next 3 turns
9. **BLOCK** - The next player must skip their turn
10. **LUCKY DRAW** - Draw another card and use it immediately
11. **REVERSE** - Reverse the game direction
12. **MIRROR** - Copy the last action card played
13. **SHUFFLE** - All players redistribute points equally
14. **LUCKY BREAK** - Gain 5 bonus points
15. **SWAP LEFT** - Trade scores with the player to your left
16. **STEAL HALF** - Steal half of a player's points
17. **RESURRECTION** - Reset negative points to 0
18. **ALLIANCE** - Team up with another player
19. **SYSTEM ERROR** - Lose 2 points (penalty variant)
20. *20th card can be customized*

### Challenge Cards (10 Total)
Challenge cards require players to solve problems, answer questions, or complete physical tasks. Success rewards points, failure may result in penalties.

**Challenge Cards:**
1. **RIDDLE** - Logic riddle (+5 on success, -3 on failure)
2. **QUICK MATH** - Solve a mathematical problem (+4 points)
3. **MEMORY** - Spell a word backward in 10 seconds (+3 points)
4. **PHYSICAL** - Perform 5 jumping jacks in 10 seconds (+3 points)
5. **TONGUE TWISTER** - Repeat a phrase 3 times fast without mistakes (+4 points)
6. **LOGIC PUZZLE** - Answer a logic question (+5 points)
7. **SEQUENCE** - Complete a number sequence (+4 points)
8. **ANAGRAM** - Unscramble a word (+3 points)
9. **TRIVIA** - Answer a trivia question (+2 points)
10. *10th card can be customized*

## üñ®Ô∏è How to Print

1. Open `cards.html` in your web browser
2. Press **Ctrl+P** (Windows) or **Cmd+P** (Mac) to open the print dialog
3. Configure print settings:
   - **Margins:** Minimal or None (for maximum card space)
   - **Paper Size:** A4 or Letter
   - **Orientation:** Portrait or Landscape
4. Click "Print" or "Save as PDF"
5. Cut along the dotted lines to separate individual cards

### Recommended Paper
- **Cardstock** (200-250 GSM) - For durability
- **White or cream-colored paper** - For better color visibility
- **Glossy or matte finish** - Prevents wear

## üéØ Game Rules (Quick Summary)

1. **Setup:** Distribute cards face down in a deck
2. **Turn Structure:** Players draw a card when they land on a special tile
3. **Action Cards:** Apply immediately or keep for later (depending on card type)
4. **Challenge Cards:** Attempt the challenge for points or face penalties
5. **Winning:** First player to reach the finish line or highest score wins

*Full game rules should be included with your board game instructions*

## üîß Customization

You can easily customize the cards by editing `cards.html`:

1. Open the file in any text editor
2. Find the card you want to modify in the `<div class="card">` sections
3. Edit the title in `<p class="card-title">`
4. Edit the description in `<p class="card-description">`
5. Save and refresh in your browser

### Adding New Cards
Copy the entire card structure and paste it at the end before the closing `</div>` tag. Update the class to `action-card` or `challenge-card` as needed.

## üí° Tips for Game Design

- **Balance:** Ensure action cards aren't too powerful
- **Fairness:** Challenge cards should be solvable by most players
- **Variety:** Mix different card types to keep gameplay interesting
- **Accessibility:** Use simple language so all ages can understand
